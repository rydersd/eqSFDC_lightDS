Story Reference

The shared fiction that threads through every record page in EQ Lightning. Use this page to understand who the characters are, what connects them, and why the narrative exists — so every stakeholder conversation stays consistent and engaging.

Why Monty Python? Using fictional, well-known characters keeps stakeholder conversations focused on structure — which fields belong where, what related lists matter, how the path component works — rather than debating whose real account data to show. The absurdity is the point: nobody argues about whether the Knights Who Say Ni should have a different industry code.

Narrative Threads Across Records

The story follows the Knights Who Say Ni as they procure colocation services from Equinix. Each record page represents a different stage or aspect of that relationship:

Account → Contact → Opportunity → Order → Case

Account → Contact: The Knights Who Say Ni Ltd is the parent organization. Sir Robin is their VP Strategic Retreat Planning — the primary contact who evaluates solutions (briefly, before retreating).

Contact → Opportunity: Sir Robin's evaluation leads to a $580K opportunity for colocation, interconnection, and shrubbery hosting. The Head Knight demands a second shrubbery as part of the deal terms.

Opportunity → Order: When the deal closes, Sir Galahad the Pure handles procurement. The order includes shrubbery hosting and must be "purified" before provisioning.

Order → Case: Post-provisioning, Roger the Shrubber attempts to mount a herring in Rack B of the LD8 datacenter, causing the infamous support case. The Bridge Keeper resolves it by asking three questions.
